cc::PaintFlags: Use log2 for HDR headroom

All various specifications use HDR headroom as log2 of the ratio of peak
brightness to the brightness of white. In this scheme This means that
"no headroom" means "headroom 0" means "SDR". It also aligns with the
concept of stops from photography.

Historically we often used the ratio of peak brightness to the
brightness of white (without the log2), and sometimes called that
headroom.

This can get confusing, so be consistent that "headroom" be log2 of the
ratio, starting in cc::PaintFlags::ComputeEffectiveHdrHeadroom. Also add
a helper in gfx::DisplayColorSpaces.
