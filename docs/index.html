Original change's description:
> media/gpu/v4l2: wait for and produce |CAPTURE_queue_| decoded pics
>
> This CL implements the (blocking) waiting for decoded pictures on
> |CAPTURE_queue_|, extracting those decoded pictures, producing the
> associated VideoFrames and recycling them when available. It
> essentially completes the decoding part, albeit there might be
> circumstances where the logic stalls.
>
> Since the wait is blocking, it happens in a SequencedTaskRunner we
> get from a pool. A convenience RearmCAPTUREQueueMonitoring() method
> does the hop to WaitOnceForCAPTUREQueueEvent() with a callback to
> TryAndDequeueCAPTUREQueueBuffers() when the event happens. Note that
> the "wait" happens only once and is re-armed from the main thread,
> which simplifies lifetime reasoning.
>
> Only weird thing really is the specific VideoFrame handling needed
> for V4L2_MEMORY_MMAP CAPTURE queue (i.e. MTK8173 Hana), where
> there's some manual lifetime mgmt needed :-(
>
> A new DecodeAndOutputAllFrames test case is added for verification,
> and it passes on MT8173 Hana and QC7180 trogdor. Anecdotally, while
> decoding, most queues are almost empty/full (encoded chunks/decoded
> frames, respectively), pointing to the current queue mgmt as more
> performant than what ToT does.
>
> Tested on hana and trogdor via:
> /usr/local/libexec/chrome-binary-tests/video_decode_accelerator_tests
> /usr/local/share/tast/data_pushed/go.chromium.org/tast-tests/cros/local/bundles/cros/video/data/test-25fps.vp8
> '--vmodule=*/media/gpu/v4l2/*=4'
> --gtest_filter=*.DecodeAndOutputAllFrames
> --enable-features=V4L2FlatStatefulVideoDecoder
>
> (this decodes all frames but skips the Flush at the end because the
> end-of-stream logic is not implemented).
