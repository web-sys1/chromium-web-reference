CSSOM: Preserve longhand ordering when expanding all property

During cssText serialization, the 'all' property may be expanded into
longhands. In that path, longhands declared after all can be emitted out
of order and separated from related declarations, which hurts shorthand
reconstruction and cssText round-trip stability.

Track longhands that appear after 'all', and keep them in the
property-set range so they are emitted together at the end. Update index
mapping and enumeration logic in `CSSPropertyValueSetForSerializer` for
the expanded 'all' path.
