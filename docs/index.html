autocorrect: unify VK and PK logics

Previously, PK and VK had a shared logic for accepting/clearing
autocorrect range. At the same time, PK had an extra logic where four
key presses could trigger accepting autocorrect range. There were few
problems with this approach:

* PK and VK experience had differences.
* Different user interactions with the aim of implicitly rejecting the
  autocorrect suggestion could trigger the 'accept' process beforehand.
  This would impact the user experience negatively, but most importantly
  provide an incorrect metric where a bad suggestion was counted as a
  good one instead. Examples of such interactions included long-press of
  Ctrl (+Backspace for deleting the suggestion or +Z for undo),
  Shift+Arrow keys to select all/part of the suggestion and
  remove/replace it, typing two chars after the suggestion (due to late
  noticing of the suggestion) and using backspace to remove the
  suggestion, and moving with arrow keys to move the cursor inside
  suggestion and mutate it.

Now, with removing PK specific logics, the autocorrect experience is the
same for both PK and VK and most of the PK specific issues mentioned are
resolved.
