Support sibling combinators and complex selector in :has()

To invalidate the subject elements of a style rule which contains
sibling combinator or complex selector in :has() pseudo class, added
new direction of walking up previous siblings in SelectorChecker
and StyleEngine.

To prevent unnecessary previous sibling traversal or invalidation,
'siblings_affected_by_has_' flag was added to the ElementRareData
to indicate that a previous sibling of the element may match a :has()
pseudo class.

And the flag 'ancestors_affected_by_has_' was renamed to
'ancestors_or_ancestor_siblings_affected_by_has_' to have a clear
meaning. It indicate that a ancestor or a ancestor's sibling of the
element may match a :has() pseudo class.
